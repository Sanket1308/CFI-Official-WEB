"A guiding light whose love shows us the way."

  • "A guiding light whose love shows us the way."



       This is the short story of Aman Ali, a proud young guy from the magnificent city of India 'KASHMIR’ Aman Ali worked as a carpenter professionally. Despite his dedication to his working life, Aman had developed severe addictions as a result of financial stress and anxiety. "Addiction is an adaptation; it is not you; it is the cage in which you live." Aman was so engrossed in it that he couldn't think about his future or his health.. One such day, while coughing early in the morning, He underwent blood spitting. When he told his wife, she became agitated, and they both went to the hospital for a health check.

       A few days later, reports claim that the patient was suffering from modest cancer signs. That was an absolutely terrible thing to even imagine. After all of this, Aman was still unwilling to give up his addictions, and when he was in his first stage of cancer, his son became his true incentive for him. Once in the morning, as Aman was smoking a cigarette, his kid approached him and talked with him, and asked him, "daddy?" "Can I have one? " The secret to permanently breaking any habit is to love something greater than the habits".



       That was the moment Aman considered not his own future, but instead the future of his kid. It struck him that if his son gets addicted to this today, what would be his future tomorrow? “A father is neither an anchor to hold us back nor a sail to take us there but a guiding light whose love shows us the way.”
