  • "The genuine reason for his new life."



       Siddharth Tyagi, a young kid from Ranchi, Uttarakhand, As a normal student in his twenties, was busy living life to the full, juggling his time between family, friends, work and other interests.Cancer is never distant from the news, and each patient has a unique narrative to tell. However, it never crossed my mind that a healthy and cheerful young man in twenties would be diagnosed with oral cancer. In 2017, Siddharth was diagnosed with oral cancer Initially he ignored the symptoms because they were usual at the time.He discovered some mouth sores under the tongue and tried a variety of over-the-counter pain relievers, which had little impact. It never clicked to Siddharth that it might be anything so terrible.His mouth gradually became more swollen and painful, and speech slurred, eating became tough, and sleep became scarce. He struggled with everyday life . Biopsies and scans discovered a tumor near the base of mouth and tongue after a short hospital stay which confirmed “mouth cancer”. After learning that he had oral cancer, he quit consuming alcohol and smoking cigarettes, as well as abstaining from all other unhealthy habits .



       On September 8, 2017, Siddharth underwent surgery. The tumor was removed along with much of the floor of the mouth, half of the tongue, some teeth, and a minor portion of the jawbone during the approximately 12-hours of operation. It took another operation though, in which surgeons de-bulked his replacement tongue and floor of the mouth (reducing their size) .This complete procedure cost was around 3 to 4.5 lakhs . During this difficult time, his parents were by his side, and he was solely encouraged and motivated by them.



       He decided to give up all of his bad habits, such as smoking and drinking alcoholic drinks, and to go green on diet and exercise on a daily basis.It has been 5 years now. Siddharth has recovered from cancer and is eager to raise awareness about mouth cancer, provide care for other patients, and raise money to fund oral cancer research.

       "In times of test, family is best," as the proverb goes. According to Siddhart Tyagi, his family and friends were his greatest supporters since they were there for him through thick and thin, and they are the genuine reason for his new life. It's all of it."
